Congruence

Introduction

In abstract algebra, a congruence relation is an equivalence relation on an algebraic structure that is compatible with the structure in the sense that algebraic operations on equivalent operands will yield equivalent elements. Every congruence relation has a quotient structure whose elements are the equivalence classes or congruence classes for the relation.

Given an integer m≥1, called a "modulus", two integers a and b are said to be a≡b(modm) (read: a and b are congruent modulo m) if m is a divisor of their difference; that is if there is an integer k such that

a−b=km
