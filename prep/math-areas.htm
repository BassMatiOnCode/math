Arithmetic is one of the foundations of mathematics. It studies arithmetic operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, exponentiation, extracting roots, logarightm) on numbers.

Number Theory

Number theory deals with the manipulation of numbers (natural numbers ℕ, integers ℤ, rational numbers ℚ).

Prominent persons that worked on number theory are Euclid of Greece, Diophantus of Alexandria, Pierre de Fermat, Leonhard Euler, Adrien-Marie Legendre, Carl Friedrich Gauss.

Geometry

One of the oldest branches of mathematics. Started with empirical recipes concerning shapes, such as line,s angles and circles.

The old Greeks introduced the concept of proofs, which require that every assertion must be proved via reasoning from previously accepted theorems (accepted results) and a few basic statements (self-evident facts without proof or postulates, or axioms).

Definitions provide a standard foundation for communication. An axiom or postulate is a mathematical statement that is considered to be true without proof. A statement that has to be proved or disproved is called a conjecture. A proof is a series of rigorous arguments employing deductive reasoning. If a conjecture is proven to be true is then called a theorem. A specialized theorem that is mainly used to prove a theorem is called a lemma.
