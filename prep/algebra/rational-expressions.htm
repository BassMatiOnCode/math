EXAMPLE 3:

x2+x x+4 x+1 x2−16 = x2+x x+4 ⋅ x2−16 x+1

At this point, we should resist to multiply the factors in numerator and denominator. Instead, it is better to factorize and then eliminate factors that cancel out each other.

= x(x+1) x+4 ⋅ (x+4)(x−4) x+1

Now we can see that the terms x+1 and x+4 can be eliminated:

= x(x+4)(x−4) x+4
= x(x−4)

Done.
