Quadratic Equations

A quadratic expression in the standard form has a second-order polynomial on the left side, and has the following form:

ax2 +bx +c = 0

with a,b,c,x∈ℝ,a≠0.

Method 1: Factorization

Consider the following equation:

x2=18−7x

First step is to bring this into the standard form:

x2+7x−18=0

Next step is to factorize the second term. The product a times c must be negative 18, the sum of a and c must be 7.

b1 b2 Σ
18 −1 17
9 −2 7

We have a nomalized quadratic expression, so the results are already the roots and we can write:

(x+9) (x−2) = 0

This expression is true if one of the terms is zero:

x1+9=0
x2−2=0

Isolating the variables gives us the solutions:

x1=−9
x2=+2

VERIFICATION:

We substitute the values for x1 and x2 for x in the original equation:

x2=18−7x
−92=18−7⋅−9
81=18+63=81

And the second solution:

x2=18−7x
22=18−7⋅2
4=18−14=4

Done.

Method 2: Difference of Squares

Example:

x2=121
x2−121=0

When we factorize, we find that 121 is 11 squared. So the solution is

(x+11) (x−11) =0

The equation is true if one of the terms becomes zero:

x1=−11
x2=+11

Done.

But there is a shorter way to get to the result. The right side is positive, and so we can directly pull the root:

x=±121=±11

NOTE that it is important that pulling the root always generates two solutions, a positive and a negative number. Both values fulfill the original equation.

NOTE that will not work for a negative right side. If normalized, that would produce a sum of squares, which cannot be factorized in ℝ.

Method 3: Quadratic Formula

x(x+2)=7

Here we have a product of the variable, so we must distribute first and then transform the equation into the standard form.

ax2+bx+c=0
x2+2x−7=0

To factor that expression we need to find two values b1 and b2 that multiply to minus 7 and add up to 2. But since seven is a prime number, the only pairs we have are {7, 1} and {−7, −1}. It is obvious that they never add up to 2.

The way to go is the use of the quadratic formula:

x1,x1 = −b±b2−4ac 2a

With a=1,b=2,c=−7 we get:

x1,x1 = −2±22−4⋅1⋅−7 2⋅1
= −2±4+4⋅7 2
= −2±32 2

At this point, we can factorize the expression under the root and reduce further:

= −2±16⋅2 2
= −2±42 2
= −1 ±22

Done.

No Solution in ℝ

12x2=13x−2

Transform into standard form:

12x2 −13x +2 =0

To get rid of the fractional numbers, we determine the least common denominator, which is 2⋅3=6, and them multiply both sides of the equation with 6:

36x2 −26x +2 =0
3x2 −2x +12 =0

We have now two options: a) use the quadratic formula to find the solutions, or b) factorize. I have chosen b).

We are looking for a product of positive 36 and a sum of minus 2. We will need negative factors. Factorizing 36 gives us the following table:

b1 b2 Σ
−2 −18 −20
−3 −12 −15
−4 −9 −13
−6 −6 0

And that's it. No way to factorize. We will have to use the quadratic formula.

x1,x1 = −b±b2−4ac 2a
= +2±22−4⋅3⋅12 2⋅3
= +2±−140 6

Here we have a problem: Roots of negative numbers are not defined. So the equation has not solution in ℝ.
