# Vektoren

A a scalar value in physics is combination of a plain number (value) with a unit. Examples: 123 m, 234 kg, 345 km/h. A vector is a combination of a scalar value with a /direction/. 

## Im 2D Raum

Komponentendarstellung mit Einheitsvektoren
¬a = ax·¬ex+ay·¬ey

Equality
Vectors are identical if their components are idenical:
¬a=¬b <=> ax=bx,ay=by

Betrag, Länge
Pythagoras:
|¬a|=sqrt(ax^2+ay^2)

Addition zweier Vektoren
Grafisch durch Parallelverschiebung und Verkettung
¬a+¬b=(ax+bx,ay+by)·(¬ex,¬ey)

Multiplikation mit einem Skalar
r·¬a=(r·ax,r·ay)·(¬ex,¬ey)
This is effectively a stretching or compression.

# Dot Produkt

Other names: Inner product, scalar product, dot product. 

The dot product is a multiplication of two vectors and produces a scalar (non-vector) value.
 
¬a·¬b=|¬a|·|¬b|·cos(arc(a,b))
¬a·¬b=(ax·¬ex+ay·¬ey)·(bx·¬ex+by·¬ey)
=ax·¬ex·bx·¬ex + ax·¬ex·by·¬ey + ay·¬ey·bx·¬ex  + ay·¬ey·by·¬ey
Wegen cos 90° = 0 ist ¬ex·¬ey=0
Wegen cos 0° = 1 ist ¬ex·¬ex=¬ey·¬ey=1
=ax·bx·1+ax·by·0+ay·bx·0+ay·by·1
=ax·bx+ay·by

In one line:
¬a·¬b=|¬a|·|¬b|·cos(arc(a,b))=ax·bx+ay·by

Some observations
1. If two vectors are colinear, their dot product is equal to their sum of products of components, as cos phi is 1 at a 0 degree angle. The dot product of a vector with itself produces the sum of square of the vector components. In a cartesian coordinate system, the dot product of a unit vector with itself produces the scalar value 1.

2. If two vectors are perpendicular to each other, their dot product is zero, because cos 90 degree is zero. Specifically, the dot product between unit vectors in a cartesian coordinate system is zero.
